# Write the Trainee Evaluation

**The job:** Draft a trainee's phase evaluation in department format from Tom's observations, ready for his review and signature.

## Boot chain (read these, in order, and you have the skill)
1. This note, end to end.
2. The trainee's note in `FTO/` — prior phases, standing observations, past evals.
3. The Writing Rules in [[VAULT-INDEX]] — department format, facts only.

## The procedure
1. Get Tom's observations for the phase: what the trainee did well, where they fell short, specific incidents that show it.
2. Draft the eval: header (trainee, phase, dates, FTO), performance by category, each rating backed by a specific observed example — no vague "needs improvement" without an incident behind it.
3. Close with a clear recommendation: advance, extend, or remediate, and what to work on next phase.
4. Check the trainee's note for patterns across phases — improving, sliding, or stuck — and flag them to Tom.
5. Save the draft to the trainee's note in `FTO/`, update the [[FTO]] index if it's a new trainee, and tell Tom it's ready. Never submit it — Tom signs.

## Quality bar
- Every rating has a specific example behind it. Fair means specific.
- Straight language — a trainee reading it knows exactly where they stand.
- The recommendation matches the body; no soft recommendation after a hard eval.
